Chandra Operations

I work on the ACIS Operations team for the Chandra X-ray Center. ACIS is the Advanced CCD Imaging Spectrometer in the Chandra focal plane which allows for both sub-arcsecond angular resolution and ~100 eV spectral resolution. For my research area of galaxy clusters, these capabilities enable precise measurements of the densities, temperatures, and chemical composition of the hot intracluster plasma. 

My main role on the ACIS Operations team is to develop software for thermal modeling of various components on the ACIS instrument. Various factors, including sunlight, electronic components, and passive cooling into space affect ACIS temperatures. 

Other items of possible interest I have worked on:

  • ACIS Anomaly Documentation 
    Documentation on various ACIS-related anomalies which have occurred, and how to respond to them if they occur again. 
  • ACIS UV Fluence Memo 
    An investigation of an upper limit to the UV fluence seen by the ACIS focal plane over the course of the entire mission from various sources, in an effort to assess the likelihood of significant polymerization of the contaminant which has built up on the ACIS optical blocking filters.
  • ACIS Real-Time Page
    A continuously updating summary of ACIS status, including recent and upcoming commands, and thermal model predictions vs. data.
